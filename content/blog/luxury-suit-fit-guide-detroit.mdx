The most expensive suit in the room still looks average if the fit is wrong. At J. Barbaro Clothiers, we focus on proportion first: shoulder line, chest drape, waist suppression, sleeve pitch, and trouser break.

## The 5 Fit Zones We Correct First

1. **Shoulders:** no divots, no pull lines.
2. **Jacket waist:** shaped, not tight.
3. **Sleeves:** shirt cuff should show around 1/4 to 1/2 inch.
4. **Trouser rise and seat:** clean without strain.
5. **Trouser hem:** slight break or no-break depending on footwear.

## Why Tailoring Still Matters in 2026

Modern mills have improved stretch fabrics, but ready-to-wear still assumes generic body proportions. Alterations are what make luxury menswear look intentional.
